Solves the refined shear deformation theory eigenvalue problem (Hamilton's principle + Navier's solution), not a curve fit. Material: Al / Al₂O₃ (Em=70 GPa, Ec=380 GPa, ρm=2707, ρc=3800 kg/m³, μ=1/3).

Free Vibration Analysis of Functionally Graded Material Sandwich Plate Using Refined Shear Deformation Theory

Predicting how manufacturing porosity shifts natural frequency, so structural margins can be set correctly from the start.

The challenge

Thermal barrier coatings in aerospace structures need to be ceramic on the hot side and metallic on the load-bearing side. A hard boundary between the two creates stress concentrations under thermal cycling that eventually delaminate the coating. Functionally Graded Materials (FGMs) solve this by continuously transitioning composition across the thickness, but manufacturing always introduces porosity, which shifts dynamic behavior in ways standard models don't capture.

What I did

Developed an analytical model for free vibration of porous FGM sandwich plates using Refined Shear Deformation Theory and Navier's solution. Modeled three porosity distribution patterns (even, uneven, linear uneven) across three sandwich thickness ratios (1-1-1, 1-2-1, 2-1-2) with material grading from aluminum to alumina following a power law. The interactive visualizer above solves the eigenvalue problem live, exactly as derived in the paper.

How performance was evaluated

Results benchmarked against Thai & Choi and Jin et al. (two independently published theories) across multiple aspect ratios and grading indices. Agreement confirmed in all cases. Published and indexed at ICAMM, available on ResearchGate and Google Scholar.
